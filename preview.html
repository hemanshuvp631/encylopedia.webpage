Adolf Hitler

Early Life (1889–1907)

Adolf Hitler was born on April 20, 1889, in Braunau am Inn, Austria. His father was a customs official, and his mother was devoted to him. He showed early interest in art but faced difficulties in his youth.

Vienna Years (1907–1913)

After his mother's death, he moved to Vienna, where he faced poverty and was rejected from art school. During this period, he began forming strong political and ideological beliefs.

World War I (1914–1918)

He served in the German army during World War I and received awards for bravery. Germany’s defeat had a major impact on his worldview.

Rise to Power (1919–1933)

He joined the Nazi Party and became a powerful speaker and leader. In 1933, he became Chancellor of Germany and soon established a dictatorship.

Rule of Germany (1933–1939)

He consolidated power, eliminated opposition, and implemented strict control over society, media, and politics.

World War II (1939–1945)

Germany invaded Poland in 1939, starting World War II. The conflict spread globally and caused massive destruction.

The Holocaust

The Nazi regime carried out a genocide that led to the deaths of millions of people, including six million Jews.

Downfall (1944–1945)

As Allied forces advanced, Germany collapsed. He remained in Berlin during the final stages of the war.

Death (1945)

On April 30, 1945, he died by suicide in his bunker as opposing forces entered Berlin.
